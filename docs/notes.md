# Reading Notes & References

## Key Papers

### Bayar & Stamm (2018) — Constrained CNNs
- **Core idea**: First conv layer constrained to suppress content, learn manipulation residuals
- **Constraint**: Filter weights sum to zero (center = -1, rest sum to 1)
- **Result**: Better generalization than unconstrained CNNs for unseen manipulations
- **Takeaway**: Preprocessing that removes semantic content forces network to learn forensic traces

### Rössler et al. (2019) — FaceForensics++
- **Dataset**: 1000 real videos × 4 manipulation methods × 3 compression levels
- **Methods**: Deepfakes, Face2Face, FaceSwap, NeuralTextures
- **Key finding**: Performance degrades significantly with compression (c40 vs raw)

### Wang et al. (2020) — CNN-Generated Images Are Easy to Spot
- **Insight**: A classifier trained on one CNN generator (ProGAN) generalizes to others
- **Why**: Shared spectral artifacts from upsampling layers (checkerboard patterns)
- **Limitation**: Does this hold for diffusion models that don't use learned upsampling?

### Corvi et al. (2023) — On the Detection of Synthetic Images Generated by Diffusion Models
- **Finding**: Diffusion models leave different artifacts than GANs
- **Spectral**: Less pronounced checkerboard; more subtle mid-frequency anomalies
- **Implication**: Cross-generator detection may fail when crossing GAN→Diffusion boundary
